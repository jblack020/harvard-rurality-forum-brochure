## Event Overview

The Harvard Rurality Forum is the inaugural conference organized by the Harvard Undergraduate Rural League, a 150+ member student organization dedicated to fostering community for students from rural backgrounds and bridging the rural-urban divide. The Forum brings together experts, academics, and student leaders to engage in high-impact discussions on rural economic development, medicine, and education. It is made possible by the President's Building Bridges Fund, a grant from the Harvard President's Office for student-led projects that bridge campus divides. HURL was the only first-year student organization to receive the grant.

The event features three expert panels (economic development, education/admissions, and rural medicine), two keynote addresses, and a special award presentation. The Reimagining the Economy Project at HKS (directed by Professor Dani Rodrik, coordinated through Rohan Sandhu) is co-sponsoring the economic development panel.

The Harvard Gazette (staff writer Christy DeSmith) has reached out to write a feature story about HURL and the Forum.

## Speaker Bios & Background Research

### Dreama Gentry, J.D.
*Keynote Speaker & 2026 Rural Representative Award Recipient*

- Founder, President, and CEO of Partners for Rural Impact (PRI), a national intermediary working to ensure the 14 million children and youth living in rural America are on a path to upward mobility.
- PRI has eight offices across the country, 350+ employees, and an annual budget of $60+ million.
- Grew up in Appalachian Kentucky; deep roots in Harlan County going back 200+ years. Describes herself as "a proud Appalachian with no desire to live anywhere else."
- Architect of the nation's first rural Promise Neighborhood (modeled after Harlem Children's Zone), and designer of a rural-focused Full Service Community School model.
- PRI has implemented and sustained four rural Promise Neighborhoods in Appalachian Kentucky and operates 130+ Full Service Community Schools in Kentucky, Texas, and Missouri.
- Before founding PRI in 2022, served as Executive Director of Partners for Education at Berea College for over a decade, growing it from a direct-service program in a single school to a regional initiative serving 50,000 children across Appalachian Kentucky.
- Received backing from major philanthropies including Ballmer Group and Blue Meridian Partners.
- By-All-Means Senior Fellow at the Ed Redesign Lab at Harvard Graduate School of Education.
- Named one of TIME's 12 Education Activists of 2012 (while at the Data Quality Campaign — note: this may be confused with Aimee Guidera; Gentry's TIME recognition is separate).
- Annie E. Casey Foundation Fellow.
- Education: B.A. in Political Science from Berea College; J.D. from University of Kentucky College of Law.
- Lives in Paint Lick, Kentucky with her husband, Hasan Davis.
- Attended the EdRedesign mixer and spoke on the Rural College Access Panel at Harvard GSE shortly before her invitation (early March 2026).
- Met Kevin Scanlan at the EdRedesign mixer; accepted the HRF invitation on March 10, 2026.
- Planning to fly to Boston for the event and depart the evening of April 11.
- Scheduling a prep call with HURL team for Wednesday, March 18, at 9:30 AM via her assistant Holly Collins.

### Nathan Ohle
*Development Panel Speaker*

- President & CEO of the International Economic Development Council (IEDC) since February 2022. IEDC is a non-profit, non-partisan membership organization with 5,000+ members globally; the largest economic development association in the world.
- Named one of "40 Under 40 Rising Stars" in economic development internationally (2017).
- Previously CEO of the Rural Community Assistance Partnership (RCAP), a national network of nonprofit partners building capacity in rural and tribal communities. Under his leadership, RCAP's budget and team nearly tripled, serving 3.4+ million rural and tribal residents.
- Former Senior Advisor at the U.S. Economic Development Administration (EDA), overseeing policy and external affairs. Served as the U.S. Department of Commerce lead on the White House Rural Council and the Administration-wide Community Solutions team. Deputy Director of the Detroit Federal Working Group.
- Created the first-ever Office of Philanthropic Engagement at the Commerce Department.
- Before federal service, worked for Michigan Governor Jennifer Granholm and as Board Relations Liaison for the Michigan Economic Development Corporation.
- Holds two degrees from Michigan State University.
- Launched the Economic Recovery Corps with six partner organizations, embedding 65+ Fellows in distressed communities (urban, rural, suburban, tribal) across the U.S.
- Spoke at HKS Taubman Center's Economic Development Seminar in October 2025.
- Resides in Falls Church, Virginia, with his wife and two children.
- Rohan Sandhu (HKS, Reimagining the Economy Project) helped connect HURL with Nathan.
- **Travel logistics (as of 3/16/2026):** Flying from Orlando (MCO) to Boston, ideally Delta 2436 at 7:00 AM arriving BOS at 10:00 AM. Return flight to DCA, Delta 5667 at 6:30 PM. Coordinated through Jamie Richards (jrichards@iedc.org).

### Jacob Hannah
*Development Panel Speaker*

- CEO of Coalfield Development since January 2024 (previously Chief Conservation Officer since 2018).
- 5th-generation West Virginian with three generations of coal mining in his family. Grew up in a tight-knit rural community of 87 residents in Mingo County, West Virginia.
- First-generation college graduate; holds a bachelor's degree from Bucknell University and an associate's degree in Business Administration from Garrett College.
- Coalfield Development is a 501(c)(3) in Huntington, WV focused on rebuilding the Appalachian economy through social enterprise creation, job training, and community-based real estate development. Operates on a "33-6-3" model: crew members work 33 hours/week in construction, 6 hours in community college classes, and 3 hours in life skills training.
- Under Coalfield's umbrella, the workforce development model has removed barriers to employment for 800 Appalachians, incubated 72 new social enterprises, and trained 3,000 Appalachians in new sectors including sustainable manufacturing, agriculture, carpentry, renewable energy, and coal mine land reclamation.
- Leads the Appalachian Climate Technologies (ACT Now) Coalition, a collaboration of 50+ West Virginia cities, businesses, universities, and nonprofits. Secured $62.8 million through the EDA Build Back Better Regional Challenge grant and $22 million in private matching funds.
- Consulted on Just Transition principles at the White House and the World Bank.
- Spoke on a White House panel on Rural Development in Appalachia.
- Rockefeller Foundation profile subject for community revitalization work.
- Previously served as AmeriCorps VISTA Group Director for Students in Need (SING) and as a researcher/intern at Bucknell's Institute of Leadership and Technology.
- Connected to HURL through Rohan Sandhu at the Reimagining the Economy Project.

### Aimee Guidera
*Education/Admissions Panel Moderator*

- Former Virginia Secretary of Education (2022-2026), appointed by Governor Glenn Youngkin as the first member of his cabinet.
- Founder, President, and CEO of the Data Quality Campaign (DQC), a national nonprofit leading the effort to empower educators, students, parents, and policymakers with data to improve student outcomes. Founded DQC in 2005.
- Named one of TIME's 12 Education Activists of 2012 for her work prodding states to provide more education data.
- Pahara-Aspen Education Fellow.
- Alumna of the Institute for Educational Leadership's Education Policy Fellowship Program.
- Previously served as director of the Washington, DC office of the National Center for Educational Achievement.
- Former Vice President of Programs at the National Alliance of Business (NAB).
- Worked in the education division of the National Governors Association's Center for Best Practices.
- Taught for the Japanese Ministry of Education.
- Education: B.A. from Princeton University's Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs; M.P.P. from Harvard Kennedy School.
- Proponent of lab schools, charter schools, data-driven education reform, and career pathway development.
- Currently affiliated with the Institute of Politics at Harvard Kennedy School.

### Chris Avery
*Education/Admissions Panel Speaker*

- Roy E. Larsen Professor of Public Policy and Management at Harvard Kennedy School.
- Studies rating and selection mechanisms with a focus on the college admissions system.
- Co-author of *The Early Admissions Game* (Harvard University Press, 2003) with Andrew Fairbanks and Richard Zeckhauser.
- PI for the National Center for Rural Education Research Networks (NCRERN), studying postsecondary readiness in rural districts.
- Published extensively on college access for low-income and rural students, including the landmark study with Caroline Hoxby, "The Missing 'One-Offs': The Hidden Supply of High-Achieving, Low-Income Students" (Brookings, 2013).
- Recent work (December 2024) on "Supporting Postsecondary Readiness in Rural Districts: Experimental Evidence from a Rural Research Network."
- Also researches test-optional admissions, AP exam score impacts, and digital messaging interventions for college enrollment.
- Education: Ph.D. in Economic Analysis from Stanford Graduate School of Business; prior degrees from Harvard and Cambridge.
- NBER Research Associate in the Economics of Education program.
- Provided a letter of support for the HURL Building Bridges Fund application (November 2025).

### Matthew Tobey, MD, MPH
*Medicine Panel Moderator & Speaker*

- Director of the Rural Medicine Program in the Mass General Department of Medicine.
- Assistant Professor of Medicine at Harvard Medical School.
- Internal medicine physician from a rural town in upstate New York.
- Founded and directs partnerships between MGH and both the Rosebud Sioux Tribe and the Indian Health Service (IHS), beginning in 2012 after a U.S. Senate report detailed systemic dysfunction in IHS facilities in the region.
- Since 2012, his team has provided approximately 30,000 outpatient visits and 2,000 inpatient visits on the Rosebud Indian Reservation in South Dakota.
- Oversaw 100+ trainee rotations and trained 10 rising rural health physician leaders.
- Directs the MGH Fellowship Program in Rural Health Leadership, a two-year fellowship pairing clinical experience on the Rosebud Indian Reservation with an MPH at Harvard Chan School of Public Health.
- Research interests include rural primary care, addiction medicine, healthcare for incarcerated individuals, and Indigenous community health partnerships.
- Published in JAMA, Journal of Healthcare for the Poor and Underserved, JAMA Dermatology, and other journals on rural and Indigenous health topics.
- Harvard College alumnus.
- Accepted the invitation on March 2, 2026, noting family visiting that weekend but availability in the afternoon. HURL confirmed the Medicine Panel aligns with his afternoon availability (tentatively 1-2 PM slot).

### Rishi Wadhera, MD, MPP, MPhil
*Medicine Panel Speaker*

- Associate Professor of Medicine at Harvard Medical School and Associate Professor of Health Policy & Management at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health.
- Practicing cardiologist at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center.
- Associate Director of the Richard A. and Susan F. Smith Center for Outcomes Research at BIDMC. Founding Director of the Section of Health Policy within the Smith Center.
- Leads an NIH-funded research program focused on health care policy, population health, and cardiovascular outcomes, with particular emphasis on rural-urban disparities.
- Currently PI on three R01 grants from the National Institutes of Health, including one specifically titled "Strategies to Improve the Cardiovascular Health of Rural Working-Age Adults in the United States" (2024-2028).
- Published research on rural-urban differences in cardiovascular mortality (2010-2022), finding that the pandemic reversed earlier progress and widened rural-urban disparities, driven especially by young rural adults aged 25-64 who experienced a 20% rise in cardiovascular mortality since 2010.
- Published research on racial disparities in chronic disease death rates in rural areas, finding that gaps between Black and white adults in rural areas improved only minimally over two decades.
- Author of 250+ peer-reviewed publications in journals including the New England Journal of Medicine, JAMA, The Lancet, BMJ, JACC, and Circulation.
- Research has directly influenced federal health policy; his expertise has been sought by senior leaders at HHS, CMS, and key Congressional committees including the bipartisan Senate Budget Committee.
- Recipient of the Harvard Medical School Excellence in Mentoring Award. Mentored 40+ junior investigators.
- Elected member of the American Society of Clinical Investigation (ASCI).
- Serves on expert advisory panels for ICER, NCQA, and as Associate Editor for JACC.
- Education: M.D. from Mayo Clinic School of Medicine; M.Phil. in Public Health as a Gates Cambridge Scholar from the University of Cambridge; M.P.P. from Harvard Kennedy School; residency and cardiovascular fellowship at Brigham and Women's Hospital.
- HKS '18 alumnus; invitation was sent to his HKS alumni email.
- Accepted the HRF invitation; Kevin Scanlan followed up on March 16, 2026, with panel details.

The 2026 Rural Representative Award will be presented to Dreama Gentry during the closing keynote/award presentation session (3:10 PM). This is a new award created by HURL for the inaugural Forum.
